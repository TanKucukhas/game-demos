how it works

  
Every critter is plain capsule/cone meshes merged into one draw call. A vertex
  shader snaps each vertex onto the smooth-min SDF surface of all shapes combined, so
  overlapping parts converge onto one blended skin — the seams simply cease to exist.
  Normals come from the SDF gradient and colors blend by SDF proximity, so lighting and
  palette flow continuously across joints. No raymarching, no skinning: cost is per-vertex,
  which is why it runs on phones.

  
Animation is 100% procedural — reactive IK stepping for 2/4/6 legs,
  a squash-and-stretch state machine for hoppers, hover-and-bank for flyers, and verlet-rope
  tails and ears whose segments are SDF primitives too, so they stay seamlessly fused while
  they flop. A character is ~15 lines of JSON.
